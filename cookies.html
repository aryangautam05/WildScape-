Cookies Policy

Welcome to WildScape’s Cookies Policy. We use cookies to enhance your experience, analyze traffic, and provide personalized services. Below are detailed explanations about all cookie-related aspects to help you understand how we manage and use this technology.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small files stored on your device by websites you visit. They carry information that helps sites remember your preferences, login status, and browsing behavior, enabling a better and more personalized experience. Cookies may be session-based or persistent, cleaning up after browsing or staying available for repeated visits.

They generally contain identifiers, timestamps, and other metadata to track interactions with the site, facilitating smooth functionality such as shopping carts, language settings, or authentication. Cookies are widely used across the internet by nearly all websites for various purposes, both essential and analytical. They contribute significantly to usability and user experience.

Without cookies, websites would not remember your activity, requiring repeated re-inputs and offering limited customization. They are a cornerstone of personalized digital services, but also require transparent management to respect user privacy.

Cookies can be managed via browser settings, allowing users to block, delete, or restrict cookie use entirely, though this may affect website performance and features. WildScape’s implementation complies with regulations to safeguard your rights and offer control over cookie use.

We inform visitors using this policy and our cookie banner to ensure clear, upfront disclosure as part of ethical data practices. Cookies are not malicious; they are functional tools whose use must be responsible and respectful of privacy.

Our goal is to leverage cookies to enhance service quality without compromising your privacy or security. Understanding cookies empowers you to make informed choices about your online presence.

Various types of cookies are utilized for different functions — from essential to marketing — each with varying privacy implications. Education about these types enables smarter consent and controls.

Devices and browsers differ in how cookies are stored and managed, necessitating adaptive policies and technical solutions from providers like WildScape.

Please consult our detailed cookie categories below for specific descriptions and purposes assigned to each.

By continuing to use our site, you agree to our use of cookies within the consent you provide, respecting your autonomy and data preferences.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

WildScape uses several types of cookies, each serving specific purposes to optimize user experience while respecting privacy:

Essential Cookies: These cookies are critical to enable basic website functions such as page navigation, login sessions, and security features. Without essential cookies, the website cannot operate properly.

Performance Cookies: These collect anonymous information on how visitors use our site, such as pages visited and error messages. They help us optimize performance and understand user behavior to make improvements.

Functional Cookies: Functional cookies remember choices you make (such as language or region) to provide enhanced features and personalized interactions during your session or future visits.

Targeting or Advertising Cookies: Used to deliver ads relevant to your interests based on your browsing behavior and demographics. They limit ad frequency and measure campaign effectiveness but require your explicit consent.

Third-Party Cookies: These are set by partners or analytics providers like Google Analytics for marketing, performance, and functionality. They operate under their privacy terms but are used in conjunction with WildScape’s services.

We classify cookies clearly in our cookie banner, offering choices to accept, reject, or customize preferences according to categories.

Understanding these categories helps you control how much data you share while benefiting from a tailored browsing experience.

Cookies on WildScape comply with regional regulations such as GDPR or CCPA, incorporating user consent and transparency.

Different cookies have distinct lifespans, some persisting for months, others only for your current session.

We regularly review and audit cookie usage to maintain compliance and uphold best privacy practices tailored to emerging standards and technologies.

3. How We Use Cookies

Interaction Tracking: Track clicks and session data to measure service and campaign success.

4. Managing and Disabling Cookies

You have control over your cookie preferences and can modify or decline cookies if desired.

Most web browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies via their settings by disabling them or clearing their storage.

Specific instructions vary by browser but generally can be found under privacy or security settings menus.

Blocking cookies may limit certain website features, impact your login state, and degrade personalized services.

WildScape provides consent controls to enable you to accept all, reject all, or customize cookie settings for essential or optional cookies.

Adjustments to cookie settings can be made anytime through our cookie consent banner or your browser preferences.

Disabling third-party cookies can reduce behavioral advertising but will not affect essential site functions.

Some cookies essential to security and transactions cannot be disabled without limiting service functionality.

We encourage regular review of cookie and privacy settings as browser and policy updates may affect available options.

Our cookie policy page and FAQ provide continuous guidance on managing cookies responsibly.

7. Cookie Duration and Expiration

We use both session cookies, which expire when you close your browser, and persistent cookies, which stay until a set expiry or cleanup.

Essential session cookies protect login states and temporary preferences during your active visit.

Persistent cookies allow us to remember your settings and preferences for future visits, enhancing your convenience.

Expiration periods vary by cookie type: marketing cookies may last months, while analytics cookies have shorter lifetimes.

You can view and manage cookies stored on your browser including their expiration dates through settings or developer tools.

Periodic clearing of cookies is recommended for security and privacy hygiene but must be balanced with usability impacts.

Cookie expiration policies comply with legal requirements and best industry practices.

WildScape periodically reviews cookie lifespans to optimize between functionality and privacy.

Users have full power to delete persistent cookies independently at any time.

Understanding cookie durations helps manage expectations about data retention and website behavior.

8. Browser Settings and Tools

You have comprehensive control over how cookies are handled through your browser or device settings.

Most popular browsers provide options to accept, reject, delete, or restrict cookies on a per-site or global basis.

Instructions differ by browser but typically reside under privacy or security menu sections.

Some advanced users employ extensions or privacy tools to manage cookies routinely and block trackers.

Enabling ‘Do Not Track’ signals your preference not to be tracked; however, not all websites honor this setting.
